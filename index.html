Pancit Recipe

Preperation Time: 10-15 minutes (Depending on chopping skills)

Cooking Time: 20 minutes

Servings: 4

About Pancit

Pancit is a beloved Filipino noodle dish that comes in many regional variations, each offering its own twist on the flavors of the Philippines. Derived from the Hokkien word "pian i sit," meaning something quickly cooked, pancit reflects the Chinese influence on Filipino cuisine. It's typically stir-fried with vegetables, meats like chicken, pork, or shrimp, and flavored with soy sauce, garlic, and sometimes citrusy calamansi for a refreshing kick. The texture and taste of pancit vary depending on the noodles usedâ€”whether it's thin and delicate bihon rice noodles or thicker, heartier canton noodles. Its savory, umami-rich flavor is balanced with fresh, bright vegetables, making it a versatile dish perfect for any occasion. Pancit symbolizes long life and prosperity, often served at birthdays and celebrations across the Philippines. With its blend of flavors and textures, pancit offers a mouthwatering taste of Filipino culture in every bite.

Ingredient List















Cooking Instructions

  1. Prep the ingredients
    • 200g bihon noodles
    • 2 tbsp soy sauce
    • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
    Soak noodles in warm water for 10 minutes then drain and set aside. Then in a separate bowl, combine the soy sauce and oyster sauce.
  2. Sear the Chicken
    Heat oil in a pan, sear until golden brown, set aside shredded.
  3. Saute the Aromatics
    • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
    • 3 cloves garlic minced
    • 1 medium onion sliced
    Heat oil in a large pan (preferably a work) over medium heat. Add garlic and onions then saute until fragrant and translucent.
  4. Cook The Vegetables
    • 1 large carrot julienned
    • 1/2 cup green beans sliced diagonally
    • 1/2 cup cabbage shredded
    Add carrots, beans and cabbage to the pan. Stir-fry for about 2-3 minutes until they begin to soften
  5. Add chicken
    Stir in the shredded chicken, soy sauce and oyster sauce. Cook for another 2 minutes.
  6. Combine Noodles
    • Chicken broth
    • Bihon noodles
    Pour in chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Add the soaked and drained noodles and toss everything together, making sure the noodles absorb the liquid. Cook for 4-5 minutes until noodles are tender.
  7. Season
    Add salt and pepper to taste
  8. Garnish and Serve
    Top with chopped green onions and a squeeze of calamansi or lemon juice. Serve hot and enjoy!
"Walang handa kung walang pancit." - Manga Pinoy, 2024

Recipe Notes

  • Marinating Chicken - If using raaw chicken, you can marinate it for 20-30 minutes in a mix of soy sauce, garlic, and a dash of pepper for added flavor before cooking.
  • Adjust your seasoning - You can definitely adjust the amount of soy sauce depending on taste preference. Little hint too! The most common filipino brand soy sauce used for this and any other cooking is by Datu Puti. If you prefer a saltier dish, add more soy sauce, or use low-sodium soy sauce for a milder flavor. For a more umami flavour feel free to add an extra table spoon of oyster sauce. Don't forget to taste your dish before serving.
  • Make it Spicy - If you like a kick of heat, add sliced chilies or a drizzle of chili oil.
  • Leftover Tip - Pancit tastes great the next day! Reheat it in a pan with a splash of water or broth to avoid drying out the noodles.
